Was President Trump’s 2017 inauguration crowd the 'hugest' in history?

President claims it 'looked like a million-and-a-half people'; press secretary defends president with 'alternative facts'

While the president took time to personally hunt down the Tweeter of the inauguration crowd photo, he also admonished the news media, saying, “We caught them in a beauty, and I think they’re going to pay a big price.”

The Claim

President Trump's 2017 inauguration was the most attended in U.S. history

The Truth

Obama’s 2009 inauguration was the most attended in U.S. history and it is clear from photos that Trump’s inauguration did not match Obama’s in crowd size

Claim Rating
False
Unclear
True

This claim is false. The vast majority of people who watched the inauguration on T.V. or looked at official photos released by the National Park Service can agree that Trump and his advisors exaggerated the size of his crowd.

The N.P.S. released official photos of Barack Obama’s 2009 and 2013 inauguration as well as Trump’s inauguration. It is evident to anyone looking at the photos that both of Obama’s inaugurations had larger crowds. But President Trump continued to refute the evidence, stating on a tour of the C.I.A. headquarters, "We had a massive field of people. You saw that – packed. I get up this morning, I turn on one of the networks and they show an empty field. I said wait a minute, I made a speech. I looked out the field was, it looked like a million, a million and half people, they showed a field where there was practically nobody standing there.”

Kellyanne Conway, one of the president’s senior advisors, also chimed in when she defended Press Secretary Sean Spicer, telling NBC’s Chuck Todd, “Don’t be so overly dramatic about it, Chuck. What – you’re saying it’s a falsehood. And they’re giving Sean Spicer, our press secretary, gave alternative facts to that.”

But there are no alternative facts about Trump's inauguration crowd size: it was in no way the largest in history, and doesn't even come close to second.
