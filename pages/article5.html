Did McDonald's award a 79-year-old grandma millions of dollars for spilt coffee?

Some claim McDonald's willingly served 'dangerously hot' coffee; jury assess burns to award damages

In 1992, Stella Liebeck took the lid off her McDonald's styrofoam cup to add cream and sugar to her coffee. What would happen next skyrocketed her into a national conversation about judicial overreach and consumer greed.

The Claim

A grandma from New Mexico intentionally spilled coffee on herself and successfully sued McDonald’s for a million dollars in punitive damages

The Truth

An elderly woman accidentally spilled McDonald’s coffee on her lap, suffered third-degree burns, and settled with McDonald’s for an undisclosed amount less than $1 million

Claim Rating
False
Unclear
True

This claim is false. In 1992, Stella Liebeck bought a cup of coffee at a New Mexico McDonald’s drive-thru and accidentally spilled the coffee on her lap while the car was parked. The 79-year-old Liebeck, who suffered third-degree burns on her legs and groin, tried to settle her case with McDonald’s for $20,000 to cover her medical expenses and lost work income, but McDonald’s refused. After offering her just $800, the case went to trial.

During the trial, McDonald’s revealed that it kept its coffee between 180 and 190 degrees Fahrenheit. McDonald’s also admitted to knowing about the risk of serious second- and third-degree burns from its scalding hot coffee for more than 10 years. Coffee served at home is generally between 135 and 140 degrees.

The jury awarded Liebeck $200,000 for her injuries, which the judge reduced to $160,000 because he found her partly at fault for the accident. The jury then also awarded her $2.7 million in punitive damages against McDonald’s because they knowingly served their coffee dangerously hot, but the judge reduced this award to $480,000. Finally – and this is the fact that has gotten lost over the years – McDonald’s appealed and settled with Liebeck for an undisclosed amount, though most personal injury attorneys agree it was for less than $1 million.

After the verdict, McDonald’s dropped the temperature of its coffee to 158 degrees Fahrenheit and added a new warning to its coffee cups: “Caution: Coffee Is Very Hot.”
