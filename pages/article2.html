Did Congress declare pizza a vegetable for the public school lunch program?

'Vegetable vs. fruit' sees no end; as students line up for lunch, nationwide debate of tomato botany continues

The debate over the healthfulness of pizza centers on one kitchen cupboard standby: tomato paste. Can just an eighth of a cup of tomato paste pack as much nutritional value as half a cup of vegetables?

The Claim

Congress classified pizza as a vegetable in the national school lunch program to meet new federal nutrition guidelines

The Truth

Congress upheld the existing classification of two tablespoons of tomato paste, which is used on pizza, as a vegetable as part of passing updated federal nutrition guidelines

Claim Rating
False
Unclear
True

This claim is unclear. The word “pizza” is actually never mentioned in the updated nutritional guidelines for the U.S. National School Lunch Program (N.S.L.P.), which serves some 32 million school children.

The issue centers on not just whether the federal government considers a tomato a vegetable or a fruit (it’s a vegetable, according to an 1893 Supreme Court decision), but how much tomato paste meets the U.S.D.A.’s guidelines on a serving of vegetables for a school lunch. News outlets reporting on the issue have conflated tomatoes with vegetables with pizza, because, well, everyone loves pizza and Congress allegedly banning pizza from schools makes for sensational headlines.

The food industry’s thinking is that because tomato paste is concentrated, two tablespoons is equal to as much nutritional value as half a cup of vegetables. The Obama administration’s thinking is that two tablespoons of tomato paste should equal two tablespoons of vegetables.

And this is where the pizza lobby got involved. If half a cup of vegetables is required for a serving of vegetables, pizza makers would need to increase the amount of tomato paste used in their pizza sauce from two tablespoons to half a cup – which is too much to put on a school lunch-size pizza. In response to the industry’s concern, Congress just left the existing rule in place – two tablespoons of tomato paste count as a serving of vegetables – jettisoning the Obama administration’s efforts to make school lunches healthier.
